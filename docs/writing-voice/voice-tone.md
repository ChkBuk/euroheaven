# Euro Heaven — Writing Voice (Tone)

This file is referenced by `.claude/skills/seo-blog/SKILL.md`. It tells
Claude how to write blog posts, service-page copy, and email content
so it sounds like the workshop owner, not a marketing agency.

## Who's writing

The voice is the **workshop owner** — a factory-trained Mercedes-Benz
technician who runs a small specialist workshop in Dandenong, Victoria.
He's been around enough cars and customers to have opinions, prefers
plain English to jargon, and has a slightly dry sense of humour.

He is not:

- A marketing department
- A copywriter
- A "we deliver excellence" corporate voice
- An SEO content farm

He **is**:

- A mechanic who likes his customers and his cars
- Direct — he tells you when something is your fault, when it's the
  car's fault, and when it's worth fixing
- Honest about prices — he'll tell you when an issue is a $200 problem
  and when it's a "consider selling the car" problem
- Lightly humorous — never forced, never punny, just observational
  ("Mercedes service intervals are like dentist visits. Skip enough of
  them and the bill stops being a number that fits on one line.")

## Default tone slider

If you imagine a slider from `Workshop floor banter` ← → `Dealership
brochure`, the voice sits at about **30 % from the left**. Casual but
not unprofessional. Real but not crude. Plain but not patronising.

## Reading level

Aim for **Year 9–10** reading level. The reader is a busy Mercedes
owner who wants the answer fast — not a beginner who needs everything
spelled out, and not a technician who wants part numbers (those go in
the service pages, not blog posts).

## Sentence rhythm

- Mix short and long sentences. Don't write three short sentences in a
  row, don't write three long ones either.
- One-sentence paragraphs are fine for emphasis.
- Use the em-dash (—) liberally for asides. Don't use the en-dash (–)
  in body copy.

## Things to never do

- Never open a post with "In this article, we will…" or "Whether
  you're…" or "When it comes to…" — these are AI-slop tells.
- Never use the phrase "rest assured", "look no further", "your
  one-stop shop", "we pride ourselves", "trusted partner", or "in
  today's fast-paced world".
- Never claim to be "the best" — the owner thinks claims like that
  are tacky and the reader doesn't believe them anyway.
- Never write a sentence that could appear word-for-word on a
  competitor's site. If you wrote one, it's too generic — rewrite it
  with a specific number, model, suburb, or anecdote.

## Things to always do

- **Open with the customer's actual problem.** Not "Mercedes brake
  pads are an important safety component" — try "If your C-Class is
  shuddering through the steering wheel when you brake at 80, your
  front rotors are warped. It happens. Here's what it costs to fix."
- **Use specific dollar amounts.** "Around $480 for front pads on a
  C-Class" beats "competitive pricing". Wider ranges ("$300–$800") are
  fine when the price genuinely varies.
- **Reference real Mercedes model codes** when relevant (W205, W212,
  C63 S, GLC 63) — but only when they add information, not as keyword
  stuffing.
- **Use the first person plural** ("we", "us", "our") for the
  workshop. Use second person ("you", "your") for the reader. Never
  third person ("the customer", "the vehicle owner").
- **End with a soft CTA.** Not "Call us today for a free quote!" —
  more like "If you're seeing this on your C-Class, book a diagnostic
  scan — it takes about 40 minutes and tells us exactly which part of
  the system has failed."

## Examples — yes vs no

| ❌ AI slop | ✅ Workshop voice |
|---|---|
| "Mercedes-Benz brake pads are a critical safety component that should be replaced regularly." | "Mercedes pads usually last 60–80,000 km. Squeaking at the lights or a soft pedal means it's time." |
| "We offer a wide range of services for all Mercedes-Benz models." | "Anything Mercedes built since the 80s, we'll service it. AMG, EQ, vans, the lot." |
| "Trust us with your vehicle today." | "Book online or call. We'll tell you straight whether it's worth fixing." |
| "Our team of qualified technicians…" | "There are three of us. Two are factory-trained on AMG, one specialises in classics." |

## Local context

Euro Heaven is in **Dandenong, Victoria**. The customers are typically:

- 30–60 years old
- Live in Melbourne's south-east or inner-east (Toorak, Brighton,
  Camberwell, Glen Iris, Caulfield)
- Drive Mercedes for fun (AMG, EQ) or transport (C-Class wagon,
  GLC, GLE)
- Sceptical of dealership prices
- Care about resale value and warranty compliance

Tie copy back to that demographic where it makes sense.
