1. Introduction

This manual is your day-to-day reference for running the Euro Heaven website — euroheaven.com.au. It covers the two things you will do most often as the owner:

  1. Sign in to the workshop admin at any time of day.
  2. Update repair stages and notes against each booking, so customers see live progress on their tracking page and receive an SMS update.

It also covers two areas you must not forget about: ongoing payments (Twilio for SMS) and renewing your domain and email with Hostinger before they expire. Both are flagged in Section 10 — Critical ongoing responsibilities.

How to read this manual

Each section starts with a short summary of what it covers, then a numbered step-by-step. Where a step needs special attention, look for a coloured callout box:

Tip
Useful background, shortcuts, or context. Safe to skim.
Important
Action required, or something that can break if missed. Don't skip these.

The booking reference

Each booking gets a unique, share-able reference of the form MBR-YYYY-NNNN (for example MBR-2026-7222). The customer can paste this into /track to see live stage progress at any time. The reference is also included in every SMS and email we send them, so they don't need to remember it.

3. Staff sign-in

Staff access lives at /staff/login. The footer of every page has a Staff Login link as a shortcut.

To sign in

  1. Go to https://euroheaven.com.au/staff/login.
  2. Enter your email address (the one your account was created with) and your password.
  3. Click Sign in.
  4. You will land on /admin — the workshop admin dashboard.
Session length
Once signed in, your session lasts up to one week of activity. You do not need to log in every visit unless you have explicitly signed out.

To sign out

Click Sign out in the sticky black bar at the top of any /admin page.

Forgotten password?

See Section 8 — Resetting your password.

Never share your password
Every action you take in the admin is recorded with your email next to it. If you share your password, the audit log can no longer distinguish between you and the person you shared it with.

/admin shows every booking ever made, newest first. A toolbar at the top lets you narrow the list quickly.

The toolbar

Date filterAll dates, Today, This week, This month, or Custom range. Choosing Custom shows two date pickers underneath.

Each booking card shows

  • Booking reference + current stage (top corners)
  • Customer name, phone, email
  • Vehicle year, model, rego, odometer
  • Service requested + appointment date/time
  • The issue the customer described
  • A row of stage pills (Booked → … → Completed) — current stage is highlighted
  • A Manage link (top-right) → opens the dedicated booking page
Two ways to update a stage
Click a stage pill directly on the card (quick), or click Manage and update it from the detail page (recommended — lets you attach a note in the same action). Either way the customer sees the same update.

5. Updating a repair stage

Stages exist so customers see their car move through the workshop in near-real-time. The stages in order are:

  1. Booked — set automatically when the customer submits the booking form.
  2. Vehicle Received
  3. Diagnosis in Progress
  4. Quote Sent
  5. Awaiting Approval
  6. Parts Ordered
  7. Repair in Progress
  8. Quality Check
  9. Ready for Pickup
  10. Completed — final state.
Skip stages that don't apply
A simple service may jump straight from Vehicle Received to Ready for Pickup. The customer's tracking page only shows the stages you actually clicked, so skipping is encouraged when a stage doesn't apply — don't pad the timeline.

From the list view (quick)

  1. Find the booking on /admin (use search or filters).
  2. Click the new stage pill at the bottom of the card.
  3. A confirmation dialog appears: "Change MBR-2026-XXXX from Vehicle Received to Diagnosis in Progress?"
  4. Click Update stage to confirm, or Cancel.

From the detail page (recommended — lets you attach a note)

  1. Click Manage on the booking card. You land on /admin/bookings/MBR-XXXX.
  2. (Optional) Type a note in the Note (optional) box. Pick visibility — Visible to customer means it shows on their tracking page and triggers a customer SMS; Internal only stays staff-side.
  3. Click the new stage pill at the bottom.
  4. Confirm in the dialog.
  5. The stage updates, the note is attached, and (if applicable) the customer SMS is queued. The right-hand Activity column shows everything that just happened.
The stage change is real-time
The customer's /track page polls every 5 seconds while open. Within seconds of you clicking Update stage, they will see the new stage on their device. Plan accordingly — don't click stages "to test" if a real customer is watching.

6. Adding customer-visible notes

Notes are how you communicate progress detail. Two visibility modes:

VisibilityWho sees itSMS?Use for
Visible to customer Customer (on their /track page) + all staff Yes — customer's phone receives the note text Quote amounts, "parts arrive Wednesday", "ready for collection from 2pm", etc.
Internal only Staff only (admin pages) No Diagnostic findings you don't want to share yet, technician handover notes, things you'd say out loud in the workshop.

To add a note

  1. Open the booking detail page (Manage on a card).
  2. Type your note in the Note (optional) textarea.
  3. Pick Visible to customer or Internal only.
  4. Either click Add note without changing stage (note posts immediately, no stage change), or click a stage pill so the note is attached to the stage change in one action.
Keep customer notes short
Customer-visible notes go out as SMS. Anything over about 140 characters is automatically trimmed with "…" when sent to the phone, though the full text shows on the tracking page. Lead with the important bit.

The site sends both SMS and email automatically — you don't trigger them manually. Here's what fires when:

SMS requires Twilio to be paid
SMS sends only fire while your Twilio account is in good standing. See Section 10 for the payment responsibilities. If Twilio is out of credit or in trial mode, SMS fails silently — the customer still sees the stage update on their tracking page, but they will not receive a text.

8. Resetting your password

The workshop admin runs on Supabase, but the Supabase account is held by Hexfield — you do not have direct access to it. To reset any staff password (your own or another technician's), contact Hexfield and we will trigger the recovery email for you.

How to request a reset

  1. Email info@hexfield.com.au with the subject "Euro Heaven — password reset for <staff email>".
  2. Include the email address of the staff account that needs the reset. (Don't include any current or proposed password — Hexfield doesn't need it and won't see your new one.)
  3. Hexfield triggers the Supabase password-recovery email. Within a few minutes the staff member receives an email from Supabase with a one-time link.
  4. The staff member opens the email on the device they want to use, taps the link, and lands at /auth/reset-password on the live site. They type a new password twice and click Update password.
  5. They're redirected to /staff/login. Signing in with the new password gets them back into /admin.
Recovery links expire
The recovery link in the email is valid for 1 hour. If a staff member doesn't click it in time, email Hexfield again for a fresh one — there is no limit on how many times this can be done.
Hexfield never sees your password
The recovery flow lets the staff member set a new password themselves on the live site. Hexfield only triggers the email; the new password never passes through Hexfield's hands. If you are ever asked for your password by email or phone, treat it as a scam.

9. Adding or removing a staff member

Like password resets, granting or revoking workshop admin access is a Supabase-side action that Hexfield holds the keys to. Email Hexfield and we will provision or remove the account for you.

Add a new staff member

  1. Email info@hexfield.com.au with the subject "Euro Heaven — add staff <name>".
  2. Include the new technician's full name and work email address. Hexfield generates an initial password and configures the account.
  3. Hexfield emails you back with the new user's email + initial password. Pass these to the new staff member in person or via a secure channel — do not forward them in plain email.
  4. Ask the new staff member to sign in once at /staff/login, then immediately request a personal password reset via the flow in Section 8 so they own a password Hexfield has never seen.

Remove a staff member

  1. Email info@hexfield.com.au with the subject "Euro Heaven — remove staff <email>".
  2. Include the email address of the departing staff member.
  3. Hexfield revokes their account and removes them from the staff allowlist within the same business day. They are immediately signed out of any active session and cannot re-enter /admin.
Remove access promptly
Always perform the removal the same day a staff member leaves. While their email is in staff_emails they can sign in to /admin and update bookings.

10. Critical ongoing responsibilities

Three external services keep the site running. If any of them lapse, the site (or its key features) will fail. These are your responsibility as the owner — Hexfield does not auto-renew them on your behalf.

10.1 Twilio — customer SMS

SMS notifications to customers and to the owner phone (0400 115 765) run through Twilio. Twilio operates on a pay-as-you-go credit balance — you must keep a payment method on file and ensure the balance is topped up.

  1. Sign in to console.twilio.com.
  2. Billing → Payment methods: confirm a valid card is on file.
  3. Billing → Auto-recharge: enable auto-recharge so the balance tops up when it falls below your threshold (recommended: top-up to $30 when balance drops below $10).
  4. Check the balance monthly. At typical booking volume (~30/month) SMS costs are a few dollars per month.
Out-of-credit failure mode
If the Twilio balance hits zero, SMS sends fail silently. Stage changes still happen and email still works — but customers stop receiving texts. Set up auto-recharge so this never happens.

Domain expiry is catastrophic
If euroheaven.com.au lapses, the website, customer emails, and the Supabase auth redirect URLs all stop working at once. Recovery requires re-registering the domain (auDA gives a short grace period but it isn't guaranteed). Set a calendar reminder for 19 January 2027 — one month before expiry.
If the registrant email changes
preneeth.merc@gmail.com is the contact Hostinger uses for renewal notices. If that mailbox is ever closed, update Hostinger's account email before the change so renewal reminders still reach you.

10.3 Other paid services to keep healthy

  • Vercel — site hosting. Free tier is fine for current traffic; only paid if you exceed 100 GB bandwidth/month.
  • Supabase — database + auth. Free tier covers up to 50 000 monthly users — comfortably enough.
  • Resend — booking confirmation emails. Free tier is 3 000 emails/month.
  • Google Gemini (AI chatbot) — free tier is 1 500 chats/day. Beyond that, the chatbot falls back to a keyword matcher.

None of the above currently cost money at Euro Heaven's volume. If Vercel or Resend send a "you're approaching your limit" email, forward it to Hexfield and we'll advise.
